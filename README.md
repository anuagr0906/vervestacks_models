## What Makes This Model

**Actual grid topology.** Transmission network extracted from PyPSA-Eur — real substations and
high-voltage corridors, not stylised regions. Every power plant and renewable zone is mapped to its
nearest bus.

**Stress-tested time representation.** Timeslices are not calendar averages — they are algorithmically
selected to capture peak-stress, low-variable-generation, and extreme-demand periods drawn from
8 760 hours of weather and load data. The model sees the hard days.

**Land-use-aware renewables.** Solar, wind onshore, and wind offshore potentials are constrained by
protected areas, slope, proximity exclusions, and competing land uses before any cluster is formed.
Supply curves reflect what is actually buildable, not what is theoretically available.

**Demand regions, not guesswork.** Electricity demand is distributed across grid buses using
population-cluster centroids mapped to the nearest transmission node — spatially consistent and
reproducible.

**AR6-aligned scenarios.** Climate scenarios follow IPCC Sixth Assessment Report pathways, covering
5 ambition levels from current-policy to net-zero.
